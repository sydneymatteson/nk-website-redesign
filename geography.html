North Korea has a diverse landscape with encompassing alpines, forests, farmlands, freshwater, and marine ecosystems. North Korea is about the size of the state of New York and is located at roughly the same latitude as Philadelphia, giving it four distinct seasons.

Topography

North Korea has a variety of mountains and rivers, and is surrounded by seas on three sides. Mountains cover 79.5% of the land, the highest is Mt. Paektu (2,750 m). Most of the mountains rise on the north and east whereas low plains stretch along the coastline in the west and south.

Climate

North Korea is blessed with about 2,780 hours of sunlight annually, recording more hours than the other countries lying on the same latitude.There are also distinct dry and rainy seasons. There's a range of different climates from the subtropical on the south coast to the subarctic in the alpine regions of the north.
